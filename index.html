How Active Parenting supports the PFCE Framework

The Head Start Parent, Family, and Community Engagement (PFCE) Framework is a road map for building strong, goal-oriented relationships with families. Active Parenting curricula are designed to help programs strengthen key family outcomes like well-being, positive parent-child relationships, and families as lifelong educators.

Family well-being

Stress, routines, and resilience

Lessons help parents build routines, problem-solve family challenges, and practice healthy communication, which supports mental health, daily functioning, and stability at home.

Parent-child relationships

Positive, developmentally appropriate tools

Concrete skills like encouragement, logical consequences, and active listening help parents strengthen attachment and reduce power struggles in ways that fit each childâ€™s age and stage.

Families as educators

Activities and take-home practice sheets give families simple ways to support learning, social-emotional skills, and school readiness in everyday moments.

Active Parenting: First Five Years

For many Head Start and Early Head Start grantees, Active Parenting: First Five Years is the go-to curriculum for supporting families with children from birth to age five. It blends video, discussion, and take-home activities to help caregivers build nurturing, structured, and developmentally appropriate homes during the earliest years.
